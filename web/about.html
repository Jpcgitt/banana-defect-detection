Banana Sorting Monitoring System

An automated platform for real-time banana quality inspection and grading, following Philippine and ASEAN export standards.

System Overview

The Banana Sorting Monitoring System automates banana grading using computer vision. A camera analyzes each banana as it passes through the sorting area, classifying it as Good or Bad. Results are logged in real-time and displayed on the monitoring dashboard.

Purpose of the System

  • Automate the banana grading process
  • Reduce manual inspection and human error
  • Improve sorting speed and efficiency
  • Real-time monitoring of sorting results
  • Consistent banana quality standards

How the System Works

01

Bananas are placed on the sorting conveyor.

02

Camera captures images of each banana.

03

System analyzes color, size, and surface condition.

04

Banana is classified as Good or Bad.

05

Results are sent to the monitoring dashboard.

Grading Criteria for Export Banana

✓ Good Banana

  • Green peel color
  • Minimal bruises or damage
  • Normal banana shape
  • Acceptable size range

✕ Bad Banana

  • Dark discoloration or black spots
  • Large bruises or visible defects
  • Abnormal shape or very small size
  • Signs of spoilage or rot

Size Classification

Size is evaluated using estimated length measurements based on the Philippine Cavendish export standard (Codex Alimentarius / Del Monte).

Basis for Grading

The system's grading standards follow ASEAN guidelines and the Philippine National Standard (PNS/BAFPS 64:2008) for banana export quality. Key post-harvest indicators include peel color maturity, absence of defects, uniform size and shape, and overall appearance — ensuring bananas classified as Good meet international market expectations.
